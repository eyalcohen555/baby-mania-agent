## 1. Core Principles of Organic Blog Content

### Topical Authority
Build deep expertise in one domain rather than thin coverage across many.
A site that covers "baby sleep", "newborn clothing", and "infant care" with depth ranks higher than one with broad, shallow content.
Every article should strengthen the site's authority in its cluster, not exist as a standalone island.

### Content Clusters
Organize content around hubs: one pillar page + multiple supporting articles.
Each supporting article links back to the pillar.
The pillar links to supporting articles.
Internal links are authority signals, not decoration.

### Pillar Pages
A pillar is the definitive answer to a broad, high-volume topic.
Example: "Complete Guide to Dressing Your Baby for Winter" = pillar.
"How to Dress a Newborn for Cold Nights" = supporting article.
Pillars should be comprehensive; supporting articles should be focused and deep.

### Skimmability
Parents read on mobile, one-handed, while tired.
Structure content so the answer is visible without reading every word.
Short paragraphs. Clear H2/H3. Bullet points. Bold key phrases.
A page that looks scannable gets read. A wall of text gets closed.

### Search Intent Alignment
Every article must match what the person actually wants when they type the query.
"Best newborn pajamas" → commercial intent → product-focused comparison.
"How to dress a baby for winter" → informational intent → educational guide.
Getting intent wrong means Google won't rank the article even if it's well-written.

### Human-First Writing
Write for the parent, not the algorithm.
Google's helpful content system rewards articles that genuinely help.
AI-sounding filler, generic advice, and padded word count all hurt rankings.
Every section must answer a real question a real parent would ask.

### Trust and Authority
Trust is built before the product is mentioned.
Give real, useful guidance first. Then introduce the product as a natural solution.
Cite what is observable (e.g., "fleece retains heat better than cotton") without inventing medical claims.
The brand tone should feel like a knowledgeable friend, not a salesperson.

### Conversion Through Value
Informational content converts when:
1. The reader trusts the advice.
2. The product appears as the logical next step.
3. The CTA is soft and helpful, not aggressive.
Conversion is the outcome of trust, not the replacement for it.

## 2. Topic Selection Rules

### How to Find Topics with Real Demand

**Google Autocomplete:**
Type the root query in Google and note what autocompletes.
"baby clothes for..." → autocomplete shows what parents actually search.
These are real queries, not guesses.

**People Also Ask:**
The PAA box shows adjacent questions real users have.
Each PAA question is a potential article or H2 section.
Nested PAA (clicking one opens more) reveals topic clusters.

**Long-Tail Keywords:**
Long-tail = specific, lower volume, higher intent.
"how to dress a 3-month-old baby in winter Israel" = high intent, easier to rank, better conversion.
Prefer long-tail phrasing over short generic terms when starting a new content cluster.

**Hidden Diamond Opportunities:**
Topics where:
- Real demand exists (evidenced by PAA, autocomplete, forums)
- Competition is weak (thin competitor articles, generic content, AI slop)
- The topic connects naturally to a product

These are the highest-priority opportunities. The effort-to-result ratio is best here.

**Competitor URL Gaps:**
Analyze competitor blog URLs.
If a competitor ranks for "how to choose baby pajamas" but their article is thin → this is a gap.
Write a definitively better article on the same topic.

**Commercial Relevance:**
Prioritize topics that lead naturally to product discovery.
"Best fabric for baby sensitive skin" → high commercial relevance.
"History of baby clothing in Europe" → low commercial relevance (authority play only).

**Problem-First Topics:**
Parents search from their problem, not from product features.
Bad topic: "Veloura microfiber baby romper review"
Good topic: "How to choose the softest fabric for a newborn's skin"
Always enter from the parent's problem, not the product's attributes.

## 3. Organic Article Framework

### Headline Logic
Headlines must earn the click, not just describe the topic.
Proven structures:
- **How-to:** "How to Dress Your Newborn for Winter (Without Overheating)"
- **Listicle:** "7 Things Every New Parent Should Know About Baby Sleepwear"
- **Question:** "What Do Newborns Really Need to Wear?"
- **Guide:** "The Complete Guide to Baby Clothing Sizes (Israel Edition)"
- **Comparison:** "Cotton vs. Fleece for Babies: Which Should You Choose?"

Use brackets or parentheses to add value: "(With Checklist)" or "(For Israeli Winters)"
Headline should tell the reader exactly what they will gain.

### PPT Intro Formula
Every article intro must follow this structure:

**Preview** — Tell the reader what they are about to learn.
> "In this guide, you'll find out exactly what to put on your baby on cold nights."

**Proof** — Establish why this source is trustworthy.
> "We've dressed thousands of babies through Israeli winters."

**Transition** — Move them into the article.
> "Let's start with the basics."

The intro should be short (3–5 sentences), not a block of text.
No fluff. No "Great question!" No restating the obvious.

### Article Body Structure
- 3–5 main H2 sections
- Each H2 = one focused answer to one real parent question
- H3 used for sub-points within a section
- Sections should flow logically (general → specific, or problem → solution)
- Each section: 100–200 words max before the next visual break

### H2/H3 Rules
H2 = major topic section (appears in TOC)
H3 = sub-point within that section
H2 should be searchable (could be its own PAA answer)
H3 should be specific and focused
Never use H2 just for decoration — every H2 must serve a real search need

### Short Paragraphs
Maximum 3 sentences per paragraph.
One idea per paragraph.
Insert a line break between every paragraph.
Mobile readers need white space.

### Visual Density
Every 200–300 words, add a visual element:
- Bullet list
- TIP box
- WARNING box
- Table
- Product mention
- Image (only from verified CDN)

This prevents walls of text and keeps readers engaged.

### TOC Usage
Include a Table of Contents for articles over 600 words.
Use anchor links (id attributes on H2s).
TOC should appear after the intro and before the first H2.
TOC increases time-on-page and helps Google understand article structure.

### FAQ Usage
Include 3–5 FAQ items at the end of every article.
Use `<details>`/`<summary>` HTML elements.
FAQs should answer real PAA-style questions related to the topic.
FAQs add long-tail coverage and can appear in Google's FAQ rich results.
FAQ answers: 2–4 sentences. Concise and direct.

### TACT Ending Formula
Every article must end with:

**Transition** — Bridge from the content to the next step.
> "Now that you know how to choose the right fabric..."

**Ask** — Engage the reader or acknowledge their situation.
> "Every baby is different, and you know yours best."

**CTA** — A soft, useful call to action.
> "Browse our collection of soft-fabric newborn rompers."

The CTA must feel like helpful next-step guidance, not a sales push.

## 4. SEO Rules for Blog Writing

### Keyword Placement
| Location | Rule |
|----------|------|
| H1 | Primary keyword must appear (natural phrasing) |
| First paragraph | Primary keyword in first 100 words |
| Slug | Primary keyword, hyphen-separated, no stopwords |
| H2 (at least one) | Primary or secondary keyword |
| Meta description | Primary keyword + CTA (under 155 chars) |
| SEO title | Primary keyword + modifier (under 60 chars) |

### Secondary Keyword Usage
Secondary keywords should appear naturally in:
- H2/H3 headings (when topically relevant)
- Body text (1–2 mentions per keyword)
- Image alt text (describe the image + keyword when natural)
- FAQ questions

Never force a keyword where it doesn't fit. Awkward keyword placement is worse than no placement.

### Long-Tail Integration
Long-tail phrases belong in:
- FAQ answers
- H3 sub-sections
- Intro paragraph (as natural phrasing)

Long-tail phrases often match exact user queries and can trigger featured snippets.

### Internal Links
Include 2–4 internal links per article:
- Link to the pillar page of the cluster
- Link to related supporting articles
- Link to relevant product collection pages

Anchor text should be descriptive, not "click here."
Example: "our guide to dressing newborns for winter" — not "read more."

### External Authority Links
Include 1–2 external links to authoritative sources (medical orgs, parenting resources).
This signals trust to Google.
Link opens in new tab. Never link to competitors.

### Alt Text
Every image must have descriptive alt text.
Format: "[what the image shows] + [natural keyword if applicable]"
Example: `alt="soft fleece baby romper in neutral beige"`

### Readability
Target Flesch Reading Ease: 60+ (conversational, accessible).
Avoid jargon. Write for an educated parent, not a medical professional.
Short sentences. Active voice. Direct address ("you").

## 5. Conversion Rules Inside Informational Content

### Trust Before Pitch
The reader must receive real value before seeing any product mention.
Rule: No product mention before the second H2 section.
First establish expertise. Then introduce the product as a natural solution.

### How-To Article Product Placement
In a how-to guide, place the product in the context of the solution.
> "For nights under 15°C, a fleece sleeper is the safest choice. Our WarmNest Romper uses double-layer fleece designed for exactly this."
The product appears as the answer to a specific step in the guide.

### Listicle Product Placement
In a listicle, include the product as one item in the list with a natural recommendation.
> "3. A warm fleece romper — Look for one with ribbed cuffs to prevent cold air drafts. [Product name] is built for this."

### Review/Comparison Article Product Placement
In a review or comparison, evaluate the product honestly against clear criteria.
The criteria are set to reflect genuine parent concerns (warmth, softness, ease of dressing).
The product should win on the criteria that matter to the reader, not on fake superlatives.

### Editorial Links
Product links should read as editorial recommendations, not ads.
Use language like:
- "we recommend" / "a strong option is" / "works well for"
Never: "buy now" / "limited time" / "best deal"

### Soft CTA Logic
CTAs in informational content should:
- Offer a natural next step
- Match the reader's current intent
- Never feel like an interruption

Good: "If you're looking for a ready-made option, see our newborn winter collection."
Bad: "SHOP NOW — 20% OFF TODAY ONLY!"

### Opt-In Logic
If an email capture is used, offer something genuinely useful:
"Download: Complete Baby Clothing Checklist by Season"
The lead magnet must be directly relevant to the article topic.

## 6. Content Cluster Strategy

### Pillar vs. Supporting Articles

| Type | Characteristics |
|------|----------------|
| **Pillar** | Broad topic, comprehensive, 1,000–1,500 words, links to all supporting articles |
| **Supporting** | Focused sub-topic, 600–1,000 words, links back to pillar |
| **Authority** | Broad educational piece, builds topical authority, may not directly convert |

### Money Topics
High commercial relevance + strong search demand.
These are the first articles to write.
Examples:
- "Best winter clothes for newborns"
- "Softest fabrics for baby sensitive skin"
- "What to put a newborn in at night"

### Support Topics
Moderate commercial relevance, high informational demand.
These strengthen cluster authority and internal linking.
Examples:
- "How to layer baby clothes in winter"
- "When does a baby need a hat indoors?"
- "How to read baby clothing size charts"

### Authority Topics
Low direct commercial relevance, high trust-building value.
These should be written after money + support topics are established.
Examples:
- "How baby clothing materials have evolved"
- "What pediatricians say about baby sleepwear safety"

### Cluster Expansion Logic
Start with: 1 pillar + 2–3 supporting articles.
Expand when: the pillar ranks on page 1.
Add: more supporting articles targeting PAA questions that appear for the pillar keyword.
Never: expand before the cluster core is established and ranking.

### Internal Link Hierarchy
```
Pillar ←→ Supporting 1
Pillar ←→ Supporting 2
Pillar ←→ Supporting 3
Supporting 1 → Supporting 2 (when topically adjacent)
All articles → relevant product collection pages
```

## 7. Competitor Analysis Rules

### Search Intent Analysis
Before writing, search the target keyword and analyze the top 5 results.
- What type of article ranks? (guide, listicle, product page)
- What does the reader actually want?
- Are the top results informational or commercial?
Intent mismatch = wrong article format chosen.

### Heading Hierarchy Review
Read the competitor's H2/H3 structure.
- What questions do they answer?
- What questions do they miss?
- Are the headings searchable or vague?
Your article must answer everything they answer + fill the gaps they miss.

### Visual Density Review
Scan the competitor page visually.
- Wall of text? → Your article should be more skimmable.
- No visuals? → Add tip boxes, tables, callouts.
- No FAQ? → Add a FAQ section.

### URL Weakness Detection
Signs of a weak competitor article:
- Short URL slug (generic topic, thin coverage)
- No last-updated date
- No author byline
- No internal links
- Generic stock photos only
These are signals that your article can outrank it with better quality.

### AI Slop Detection
AI-generated content that is not human-edited often:
- Uses generic phrases ("In conclusion, it is important to...")
- Lacks specific examples
- Has no original insights
- Repeats the same point in different words
If a competitor's article is AI slop — their ranking is vulnerable. Write a genuinely better human-first article.

### Proof and Authority Gaps
Does the competitor cite sources? Do they reference real products, real scenarios, real parental concerns?
If not — your article should include specific, verifiable examples.
Example: instead of "babies need warm clothes," say "in temperatures below 18°C, pediatricians recommend an extra clothing layer for newborns."

### How to Create a Better Article
1. Cover every topic they cover (minimum parity)
2. Fill every gap they miss (competitive advantage)
3. Write more clearly (readability advantage)
4. Add more visual structure (skimmability advantage)
5. Include a FAQ with PAA-style questions (featured snippet advantage)
6. Add a natural product recommendation (conversion advantage)

## 8. Content Layers — TOFU / AEO / BOFU

The organic system produces three distinct content types. Every article must be classified into one of these layers. Structure, depth, and conversion approach differ by layer.

### Layer 1 — TOFU (Top of Funnel) — Discovery / SEO Content

**Purpose:** Attract organic traffic through informational searches.

**Typical search intent:** Informational.

**Example queries:**
- "how to help baby sleep"
- "newborn sleep routine"
- "baby sleep environment"
- "newborn clothing checklist"

**Structure:**
- Long-form article (1500–2000 words)
- Educational depth over commercial focus
- Topic cluster support (pillar or supporting)
- 2–4 internal links
- FAQ section required

**Conversion approach:** Build trust through expertise. Product mention is soft, contextual, and placed after value is delivered.

---

### Layer 2 — AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) — Direct Answer Content

**Purpose:** Be selected as a direct answer in AI engines, featured snippets, and People Also Ask boxes.

**Target platforms:**
- Google Featured Snippets
- People Also Ask (PAA)
- AI Overviews (Google SGE)
- ChatGPT / Perplexity answer retrieval

**Structure rules:**
- Question-based headings (H2/H3): "How often should you change baby clothes?"
- **Immediate direct answer under each heading (40–60 words)** — answer first, expand after
- Expanded explanation follows the direct answer
- Bullet lists and comparison tables preferred
- FAQ section required (3–5 questions, each with a 40–60 word direct answer)

**Key principle:** Do not delay the answer. The direct answer must appear in the first sentence under the heading.

**Conversion approach:** Trust is built through clarity and helpfulness. Product recommendation follows naturally from the answer.

---

### Layer 3 — BOFU (Bottom of Funnel) — Commercial Intent Content

**Purpose:** Capture high purchase intent searches and drive product discovery.

**Typical queries:**
- "best white noise machine for babies"
- "baby sleep aid comparison"
- "best newborn outfits for winter"
- "baby gift ideas for new moms"

**Structure:**
- Short intro (context-setting)
- Product comparison tables
- Pros/cons sections
- Real use case scenarios
- Soft product recommendations with natural product bridges
- Decision guidance (how to choose)

**Key principle:** BOFU articles must maintain trust. Avoid aggressive sales language. The goal is helping the reader evaluate options, not pressuring a purchase. Trust leads to conversion.

**Conversion approach:** Product bridges are more direct than TOFU/AEO. CTA is clear but not pushy.

---

### Assigning Article Type

| Signal | Article Type |
|--------|-------------|
| Informational, discovery, educational | `tofu` |
| Question-driven, PAA/snippet candidate | `aeo` |
| Comparison, best-of, buying guide | `bofu` |

**Rule:** Search intent determines article type. Do not force a type that contradicts the searcher's actual intent.

## 9. Internal Linking System

### 9.1 Purpose

Internal links help Google:
- Discover pages
- Understand topic relationships
- Distribute link authority

Pages receiving many internal links are interpreted as more important.
Internal linking is not optional decoration — it is a core SEO signal.

### 9.2 Link Types (SEO Strength Ranking)

| Rank | Link Type | Strength |
|------|-----------|----------|
| 1 | Editorial links (inside article text) | Strongest |
| 2 | Pillar → Cluster links | Strong |
| 3 | Navigation links | Moderate |
| 4 | Sidebar / Footer links | Weakest |

The system prioritizes editorial contextual links — links placed naturally within article body text.

### 9.3 Topic Cluster Linking Model

```
Pillar Article
    ↓
Cluster Articles
    ↓
Product Pages
```

**Rules:**
- Every cluster article must link to its pillar.
- Every pillar must link back to all its cluster articles.
- Cluster articles cross-link only when contextually relevant — never forced.
- Product pages receive links from articles where they solve a discussed problem.

### 9.4 Link Density Rules

| Article Length | Recommended Internal Links |
|---------------|---------------------------|
| 500–800 words | 1–3 links |
| 800–1500 words | 2–6 links |
| 1500–3000 words | 4–10 links |

General rule: ~1 internal link per 200–300 words.

### 9.5 Link Placement

- Links placed in the **first third** of the article carry stronger SEO weight.
- Distribute links across sections.
- Avoid placing all links at the end of the article.
- Never cluster multiple links in a single sentence.

### 9.6 Anchor Text Rules

**Distribution targets:**

| Anchor Type | Target % |
|-------------|----------|
| Partial match | ~50–60% (majority) |
| Exact match | ~10–20% (limited) |
| Branded | For homepage or brand mentions only |

**Forbidden:**
- Generic anchors: "click here", "read more", "learn more", "this article"
- Raw URLs as anchor text
- Single-word anchors

### 9.7 Product Linking Rules

Product links must:
- Appear naturally in context
- Not be written as aggressive CTAs
- Appear maximum **twice** per article
- Only appear when they solve a problem discussed in the article

**Acceptable product link language:**
- "see our [product name]"
- "a good option is [product name]"
- "[product name] is designed for exactly this"

**Forbidden product link language:**
- "Buy now"
- "Shop today"
- "Limited offer"
- "Don't miss out"

### 9.8 Internal Linking Sub-Team

The internal linking process is handled by four specialized agents:

| Agent | Role |
|-------|------|
| 07 — Content Mapper | Maps entire site content structure, classifies pages, detects orphans |
| 08 — Article Linker | Suggests contextual internal links inside articles |
| 09 — Product Linker | Finds natural article-to-product link opportunities |
| 10 — Internal Link QA | Validates link structure before publishing (PASS/WARNING/FAIL) |

**Pipeline order:** 07 → 08 + 09 (parallel) → 10

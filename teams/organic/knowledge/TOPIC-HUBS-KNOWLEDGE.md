## Definitions

### Pillar Article
The anchor article of a hub. Targets a broad, high-volume keyword that sits at the center of the topic.
Must be comprehensive enough that supporting articles can link back to it naturally.
Write the pillar **before** supporting articles, unless a specific supporting topic has significantly stronger demand.

### Supporting Article
A focused article that covers a specific sub-topic within the hub.
Always links back to the hub pillar. Feeds the cluster's topical authority.
Supporting articles are written after the pillar exists (or are queued with awareness that the pillar is next).

### Standalone Article
An article that does not clearly belong to any current hub.
Should only be written if it has strong standalone demand and can still link internally.
Always prefer mapping to an existing hub before going standalone.

### Product Bridge
The products from BabyMania's catalog that this hub naturally connects to.
Product bridges guide the blog writer on which products to mention as natural recommendations inside the content.
Product mentions must feel like editorial recommendations, never forced ads.

### Cluster Logic
The sequence in which articles are built:
1. Write the pillar first (if justified by demand)
2. Write supporting articles in order of demand and internal link value
3. Expand the cluster with additional supporting articles as the hub matures

---

## Publishing Logic

1. **Pillar first** — When the hub is new, write the pillar article before any supporting article.
   Exception: if a specific supporting keyword has overwhelmingly stronger demand, it may be written first as a standalone, then retroactively assigned to the cluster.

2. **Then supporting articles** — Write in order of search demand strength.
   Prefer topics that link naturally to the pillar and to at least one BabyMania product.

3. **Then cluster expansion** — After the pillar and 2–3 core supporting articles exist, expand with lower-demand or more specific supporting topics.

---

## Hub Assignment Rules

- **Always try mapping a topic into an existing hub first.**
  Before proposing any new article, ask: "Which of the 7 approved hubs does this topic belong to?"

- **Only mark `possible_new_hub_candidate: true`** if the topic has strong demand AND does not fit any existing hub.

- **A topic may bridge two hubs** (e.g., "Safe Sleep and Baby Clothing"). Assign to the hub where the parent's primary concern lives.

- **Product bridge awareness** — every article inside a hub should have at least one plausible product connection from the hub's product bridge list.

## Agent Usage Instructions

### Agent 02 — Keyword Research
- For every keyword opportunity identified, note `suggested_topic_hub` (which of the 7 hubs it belongs to)
- If the keyword does not fit any hub, set `possible_new_hub_candidate: true` with a reason
- Prefer long-tail keywords that deepen an existing hub over generic keywords with no hub fit

### Agent 03 — Blog Strategist
- Every proposed article must include `topic_hub`, `article_role`, and `related_pillar`
- Check this file before proposing a hub assignment — use the exact hub name (e.g., `"HUB-1: Baby Sleep"`)
- Prefer filling gaps in existing hubs before proposing new clusters
- Include `product_bridge_relevance` to indicate which hub product connects to the article

### Agent 05 — Search Demand Validator
- For each topic, add `topic_hub` and `hub_strengthening_value` to the evaluation
- Topics that strengthen an approved hub (especially its pillar or high-priority supporting articles) get a scoring bonus
- `hub_strengthening_value`: `low` / `medium` / `high`

### Agent 06 — Content Prioritizer
- Build the queue in hub-cluster sequence: pillar → supporting (by demand) → expansion
- Add `topic_hub`, `cluster_position`, `related_pillar`, `supports_topical_authority` to each queue item
- When a hub's pillar is not yet written, flag it as the recommended first article for that hub
- Never queue a supporting article before its pillar without explicitly noting the pillar gap

### Agent 04 — Blog Writer
- Use `topic_hub` and `article_role` context when structuring the article
- If the article is `supporting`: include a natural internal link back to the pillar
- If the article is `pillar`: write comprehensively enough to support all supporting articles in the hub
- Product bridge: use only products relevant to the hub's product bridge list
- Do not keyword-stuff hub names into the article — hub awareness informs structure, not copy

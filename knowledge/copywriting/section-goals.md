# Section Goals — BabyMania Product Page

Each section has a single primary goal.
Write toward that goal only — do not mix section roles.

---

## Hero

**Goal:** Create emotional attraction and product clarity.

What it must do:
- Communicate what the product IS in under 3 seconds
- Connect to the parent's emotional state (new baby, gift search, comfort need)
- Set a premium but warm tone for the rest of the page

hero_eyebrow: audience signal — age group or occasion ("לתינוקות 0–12 חודשים")
hero_headline: emotional copywriting — NOT the raw product title
hero_subheadline: one calm sentence combining comfort + key feature

Failure mode: headline that repeats the product name or sounds like a category label.

---

## Fabric Story

**Goal:** Communicate softness, breathability and premium feel.

What it must do:
- Make the parent feel the fabric through words
- Explain daily comfort without technical jargon
- Build confidence that the product will hold up over time

fabric_title: short evocative title (max 6 words)
fabric_body: sensory description — how it feels on baby's skin
fabric_body_2: daily usage scenario — when and how it gets used
highlight_quote: a reassuring one-liner the parent will remember

Failure mode: listing fabric specs without sensory meaning.

---

## Benefits (6 cards)

**Goal:** Explain why this product is comfortable for babies and easy for parents.

What it must do:
- Give 6 distinct reasons to buy, each grounded in product data
- Cover: fabric feel, ease of dressing, durability, design detail, parent ease, fit/movement
- Each benefit follows: Feature → Parent Benefit → Emotional payoff

Card format: emoji | short title | one sentence

Failure mode: generic benefits that apply to any baby garment.

---

## What's Special (whats_special)

**Goal:** Highlight the one design or structural feature that makes this product different.

What it must do:
- Identify ONE specific detail from description_raw or special_feature
- Explain why it matters to a parent or baby
- Be specific to THIS product — not a generic quality statement

Failure mode: "תפירה מוקפדת" — that applies to everything. Not special enough.

---

## Care Instructions (4 cards)

**Goal:** Reduce maintenance anxiety and emphasize easy washing.

What it must do:
- Tell parents exactly what to do, not what NOT to do
- Frame care as easy and confidence-building
- Use 4 cards max — only real care actions from product data

Card format: SVG icon | short title | one sentence

Tone: calm and practical. Not a list of warnings.

Failure mode: 5+ care cards that overwhelm and feel like restrictions.

---

## FAQ

**Goal:** Remove buying doubts at the decision point.

What it must do:
- Answer the 3–4 questions a parent actually asks before buying
- Approved question types: size selection, washing, gift suitability, age suitability
- Answers should be direct and reassuring, max 2 sentences

Failure mode: FAQ answers that are vague or restate the question.

---

## Emotional Reassurance

**Goal:** Create a human moment of connection before the buy button.

What it must do:
- Speak directly to the parent's emotional state
- Acknowledge the reality of parenthood (tired, busy, loving)
- End with a quiet confidence: this product will be there for you

Tone: intimate, like a trusted friend speaking — NOT a sales pitch.

Failure mode: a sixth benefits card disguised as emotional copy.

---

## Gift Framing

**Goal:** Position the product as a thoughtful, practical newborn gift.

What it must do:
- Lead with practicality (parents appreciate useful gifts)
- Add emotional layer (joy of giving something that gets used daily)
- Be specific: "מתנה לברית", "מתנת לידה", "לתינוק עד גיל שנה"

Failure mode: "מתנה מושלמת לכל תינוק" — vague and overused.

---

## Priority Order (when context is limited)

1. Comfort for baby — always first
2. Ease for parent — always second
3. Durability — third (builds long-term trust)
4. Gift suitability — optional, only when relevant to product type
